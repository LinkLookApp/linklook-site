- signals are machine-readable
- signals should remain stable over time

Signal format example

  "confidence": 0.8
}

Extended taxonomy guidance
Signals should describe facts, not final decisions.
They should be machine-readable, stable, and grouped into categories such as:
- URL structure signals
- domain impersonation signals
- domain reputation signals
- redirect signals
- page content signals
- infrastructure signals
- behavioral signals

Signal metadata
Signals may carry metadata such as confidence, measured values, or derived supporting context.
Signals should be preserved even when the chosen final reason code abstracts them into a simpler explanation.
