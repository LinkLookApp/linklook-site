LINKLOOK — DISTRIBUTION AND DISCOVERY STRATEGY
Version: 2026-03-08
Origin: ChatGPT

OBJECTIVE
Ensure LinkLook is easy to find when someone searches for protection after a
near-scam event.

CHANNELS

1. App Store search
Most important discovery surface for motivated iPhone users.

Recommended keyword focus:
- phishing protection
- scam protection
- link checker
- phishing detector
- safe links

Suggested positioning line:
LinkLook checks links before they open and warns if the destination looks
suspicious.

2. Website search capture
Create simple plain-language pages for high-intent searches:
- how to check if a link is safe on iPhone
- SMS scam link iPhone
- what happens if you click a phishing link
- protect parents from scams
- phishing protection iPhone

3. Editorial / PR
Aim for stories around:
- protecting elderly relatives from scam links
- reducing smishing risk on iPhone
- privacy-safe alternative to heavy mobile security products

4. Word-of-mouth
Support near-miss storytelling with credible warning screens and optional,
subtle sharing prompts.

IMPORTANT COPY RULE
The product should always be discoverable through problem language, not only
through technical security language.


NETHERLANDS-FIRST EXECUTION
Use the Netherlands as the first concentrated launch market.
Reasons:
- manageable national market for early trust-building
- strong local media amplification
- clear consumer scam problem already recognised
- English-friendly environment reduces launch friction

Priority Dutch discovery / credibility surfaces:
- Tweakers and adjacent tech communities
- Dutch scam-awareness media and consumer-protection coverage
- family / elderly protection narratives

PRESS HEADLINE DIRECTION
Prefer practical headlines such as:
- Dutch app warns before scam links open
- New app helps protect elderly people from scam links
- This app checks links before they open
