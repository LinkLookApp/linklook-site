LinkLook Trust Speed Friction Principles
Version: v1.0
Date: 2026-03-08
Origin: ChatGPT

Purpose
Define the psychological UX requirements that LinkLook must satisfy to remain trusted and usable.

Principle 1: Speed
Users are highly sensitive to delay in link-opening flows.
LinkLook should aim for a near-instant experience on safe paths.
Safe links should feel checked, not slowed down.

Principle 2: Trust
Users should feel protected, not confused or manipulated.
Trust is supported by:
- calm presentation
- clear reasons
- consistent behavior
- honest handling of uncertainty

Principle 3: Low friction
A security product that interrupts too often will be bypassed or ignored.
LinkLook should minimize friction on safe links while preserving strong protection on risky ones.

Measurement themes
- tap-to-decision time
- warning obedience rate
- override rate
- trust and reassurance feedback
- false-positive tolerance

Design implication
LinkLook should feel like a calm security guard by the door, not a police officer stopping the user every few seconds.
