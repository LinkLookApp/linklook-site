LinkLook Human Usability Test Plan
Version: v1.0
Date: 2026-03-08
Origin: ChatGPT

Purpose
Define how LinkLook validates whether real users understand and follow its warnings.

Why human testing is essential
Automated UI tests can verify that screens appear and buttons work.
They cannot verify whether users actually understand the warning, trust the app, or behave safely under pressure.

Primary research questions
- Do users understand what LinkLook is telling them?
- Do users obey strong warnings?
- Do users feel protected rather than confused or blocked?
- Do safe-link checks feel fast and low-friction?
- How much warning frequency can users tolerate before fatigue appears?

Priority user groups
- general smartphone users
- lower-confidence digital users
- elderly users or users with limited technical vocabulary

Core usability scenarios
1. Safe link
Observe whether the check feels fast and reassuring.

2. Known phishing warning
Observe whether the user understands the danger and chooses the safe action.

3. Suspicious but uncertain warning
Observe whether the user understands uncertainty and avoids overtrusting or undertrusting the system.

4. Network failure
Observe whether the user understands that the link could not be verified.

5. False positive style case
Observe trust retention when LinkLook is cautious but the user expected a safe site.

6. Shortened link expansion
Observe whether transparency about the real destination improves understanding.

Metrics
- warning comprehension
- warning adherence / obedience rate
- override rate
- decision time
- trust rating
- frustration rating
- false positive tolerance

Suggested observation prompts
Ask users after each scenario:
- What do you think LinkLook is telling you?
- What would you do next?
- Why?
- How certain do you feel?
- Did the app feel helpful or annoying?

Success indicators
- users can explain the warning in their own words
- high obedience for strong warnings
- safe links feel lightweight
- users do not feel trapped during uncertain cases
- users can distinguish confirmed risk from incomplete analysis

Design implications
Findings from usability sessions should directly update:
- warning copy
- button hierarchy
- severity thresholds
- educational microcopy
- warning frequency policies
