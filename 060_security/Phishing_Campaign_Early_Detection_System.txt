Phishing Campaign Early Detection System
Generated: 2026-03-08

Purpose
Describe how LinkLook can detect phishing infrastructure and campaign patterns before large-scale victim impact.

Core idea
Do not wait for confirmed victim reports alone.
Look for weak early signals that become powerful when correlated.

Signal families
- newly registered domains
- recently first-seen certificates
- brand similarity to protected domains
- suspicious keyword patterns such as login, verify, billing, support, secure
- clusters of related domains using similar naming conventions
- shared hosting or IP infrastructure
- redirect chains converging on similar targets
- shortener links resolving into new suspicious domains
- AASA anomalies or suspicious app-link mappings
- repeated appearance of similar form structures

Detection pipeline
1. collect domain and infrastructure observations
2. correlate against protected brand dataset
3. identify clusters
4. score campaign likelihood
5. publish high-confidence campaign indicators to detection engine
6. review false positives and refine thresholds

Campaign cluster example
- secure-postnl-track.example
- postnl-billing-check.example
- postnl-secure-update.example

Possible outputs
- campaign_id
- suspected_target_brand
- related_domains
- infrastructure_overlap
- campaign_confidence
- first_seen
- recommended_action

Actions
- raise risk for all linked indicators
- prioritize analyst review
- enrich the block / suspicious decision layer
- feed model training and test cases

Important boundary
Early detection should bias toward correlation and review, not automatic hard blocking of every weak signal.
