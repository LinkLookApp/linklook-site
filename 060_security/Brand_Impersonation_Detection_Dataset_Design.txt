Brand Impersonation Detection Dataset Design
Generated: 2026-03-08

Purpose
Define the dataset that powers LinkLook's brand impersonation, domain similarity, and typosquatting detection.

Why this matters
A large share of phishing attacks imitates trusted brands, banks, government portals, delivery services, and support workflows.
Without a curated protected-brand dataset, detection quality will be inconsistent and harder to explain.

Dataset scope
Priority categories
- major technology platforms
- payment providers
- major Dutch banks
- major EU banks
- Dutch government and tax portals
- parcel and delivery brands
- telecom providers
- identity and login platforms
- high-value consumer brands frequently abused in scams

Core record structure
- brand_id
- brand_name
- canonical_domains
- known_legitimate_tlds
- known_login_domains
- brand_keywords
- protected_paths
- region
- category
- risk_tier
- notes
- source
- last_reviewed

Example record
brand_name: PayPal
canonical_domains:
- paypal.com
known_legitimate_tlds:
- .com
brand_keywords:
- paypal
- payment
risk_tier: high

Governance rules
- every entry should have a source and review date
- changes should be versioned
- risky additions should support manual approval
- false-positive suppressions should be recorded separately
- regional variants should be explicit rather than implied

Consumers
- Domain_Similarity_Detection.txt
- Typosquatting_Detection.txt
- Detection_Engine_Scoring_Model.txt
- First_24_Hours_Domain_Risk_Model.txt

Operational note
This dataset is part of LinkLook's core defensive value and should be treated as security intelligence, not as a generic marketing list.
