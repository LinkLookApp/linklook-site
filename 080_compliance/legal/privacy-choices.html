Privacy choices in LinkLook

Last updated: March 18, 2026

LinkLook gives you full control over what data leaves your device. You choose one of three protection levels during setup, and you can change your choice at any time.

📱 On-device only Default

Everything stays on your iPhone.

No data is sent to LinkLook’s servers or any third party.

LinkLook’s built-in engine analyzes the link structure locally. This catches many scam patterns without any cloud connection.

☁️ Cloud link check

The link you check is sent to the LinkLook cloud for AI analysis.

The website behind the link is not fetched or opened.

Our AI examines the web address for patterns that the on-device engine might miss. The actual webpage is never visited by our servers.

☁️ Cloud link & page check

The link is sent, and the website is fetched and analyzed by LinkLook’s server.

The page is only viewed by our server — it is not stored permanently.

Strongest protection. Our AI reads the page looking for fake login forms, brand impersonation, and social engineering. The page content is processed in memory and discarded after analysis.

How to change your choice

Open LinkLook → tap the LinkLook logo (bottom-left) → Settings → Enhanced Cloud Protection. Toggle any option on or off. Changes take effect immediately.

Google Safe Browsing

In addition to LinkLook’s own checks, the app queries the Google Safe Browsing API using partial URL hashes (not full URLs). This check runs regardless of your cloud protection choice and helps detect known malicious sites. Google’s Privacy Policy applies.

Sensitive data detection

Before any URL leaves your device, LinkLook scans it for sensitive information (emails, phone numbers, tokens, health data). If detected, you’re warned and can choose to mask, skip, or proceed. This happens on-device, before any cloud transmission.
