Split Second Support

One day, my 90-year-old mother called me. Her voice was shaky. She was
confused and worried about a transaction she didn’t recognise.

My heart dropped. I was sure she’d been scammed.

And it wouldn’t have been her fault.

We sat down, logged into her account, and went through it together. In
the end, it was a false alarm. The relief was enormous.

But that moment stayed with me.

I’ve worked in security-focused IT for years. And yet, I still sometimes
tap a link and only afterwards think, “What did I just do?”

It happens in an instant. We’ve all had that moment.

And that automatic, almost routine tap — before we’ve really thought —
is where things can go wrong.

After that call with my mum, I went looking for an app that protects
people in that exact moment — one that gives you that split second back.

I couldn’t find one. So I started building it myself.

LinkLook:

Look before you link. Link only when it’s safe.

LinkLook works quietly in the background. Tap a link — in any app,
anywhere — and it’s checked instantly.

If the link is safe, everything carries on as normal. If the link is
dangerous, LinkLook steps in — in the way you’ve chosen.

I’m building LinkLook as we speak, aiming for the App Store in 2026.

This isn’t about telling people to be more careful. It’s about taking
care of people.

If this resonates with you, I’d love to connect.
