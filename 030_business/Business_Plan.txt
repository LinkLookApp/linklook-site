================================================================================
1. SUMMARY
================================================================================

LinkLook is an iPhone security product that checks links before they open.
Its strategic advantage is timing: it intervenes at the tap moment, before the
user lands on the destination page. That makes the product unusually well suited
for phishing, smishing, parcel scams, fake bank alerts, and similar mobile-first
attacks.

The company thesis is simple:
- Scam attacks increasingly start with mobile link taps.
- Users often act too quickly.
- Existing protections usually operate later in the chain.
- LinkLook introduces a pause, evaluates the destination, and explains the risk.

LinkLook is therefore not a generic “mobile security app.” It is a focused
pre-navigation link safety layer.

================================================================================
2. CORE POSITIONING
================================================================================

Primary plain-language promise:
“LinkLook checks links before they open.”

Supporting promise:
“LinkLook reduces the risk of scam and phishing links on iPhone.”

Language to avoid:
- “Prevents scams”
- “Stops all phishing”
- “Complete protection”
- Any absolute claim that creates a reputational trap

Core category to build in the user’s mind:
- link safety layer
- scam link protection
- phishing link checker

================================================================================
3. PRODUCT SCOPE AND LAUNCH LOGIC
================================================================================

3.1 Straightforward, security-driven browser (V1)

Recommended first public product:
- straightforward, security-driven browser
- link classification pipeline with four-state outcome model
- plain-English warning UX
- built-in browsing flow inside LinkLook after verdict
- visible but calm activity summaries

Planning estimate for protection value:
- V1 browser: roughly 60–75% reduction in scam exposure for the average
  iPhone user, assuming the detection pipeline is competent and the main
  scam path is browser-opened links

This estimate is a business-planning approximation, not a public claim.

3.2 Version roadmap

- V2: Action extension via share sheet
- V3: Network Extension

Network Extension remains strategically important because it extends visibility
and closes part of the browser-bypass gap. However, it materially increases:
- engineering complexity
- entitlement and review risk
- privacy-review scope
- testing scope
- launch delay risk

Planning estimate:
- V1 browser-only: baseline effort
- browser + NE in the same first release: roughly 2–4x the execution effort

Working product strategy:
- NE is not a gate for first validation — it is V3
- AI comes before Network Extension in the roadmap (MDL-088)

3.3 AI in v1

AI belongs in v1 as a classification component, not as an autonomous “agent”
product layer. The architecture should be:
- deterministic and rule-based checks first
- domain and redirect intelligence second
- AI analysis for ambiguous or higher-risk cases

The product is “security platform with AI,” not “AI product that happens to do
security.”

================================================================================
4. REPUTATION STRATEGY
================================================================================

The real formula is:
reputation risk = expectation - reality

A miss does not automatically destroy trust. Security products miss attacks.
Trust is damaged primarily when marketing implies certainty that the architecture
cannot honestly deliver.

Reputation protection rules:
- promise risk reduction, not perfection
- prefer warnings over aggressive hard blocking when confidence is not high
- explain limitations calmly and honestly
- make visible what LinkLook did check, even when everything looked safe
- be extremely clear that the product is privacy-minimised, not surveillance

Three early reputational failure modes to avoid:
1. Looking like spyware
2. Blocking legitimate links too often
3. Looking inactive or invisible after installation

================================================================================
5. TARGET USERS
================================================================================

Primary strategic user group:
Adult children and family members who want to protect elderly relatives from
scam links.

Why this group matters:
- the emotional motivation is high
- the user/buyer split works in LinkLook’s favour
- scam stories circulate strongly in families
- word-of-mouth can be more natural than in generic security software

Secondary groups:
- security-curious iPhone users
- people who recently experienced a near-scam event
- privacy-conscious users who dislike VPN-style surveillance products

================================================================================
6. GO-TO-MARKET
================================================================================

6.1 Free v1

v1 should remain free as an intentional adoption strategy.

Rationale:
- removes purchase friction at the exact moment users want reassurance
- improves trial willingness for a new trust-sensitive app
- increases telemetry and feedback volume
- supports word-of-mouth and PR
- helps family members recommend the app to relatives without pricing friction

Principle:
Early-stage LinkLook optimises first for installs, trust, and validated demand;
not for near-term revenue extraction.

6.2 Discovery channels

Most important first channels:
1. App Store search
2. Simple website pages targeting problem-solution search intent
3. Near-miss sharing between family, friends, and colleagues
4. PR / editorial coverage around scam prevention and elderly protection

6.3 Search-intent strategy

LinkLook should be easy to discover when a person searches immediately after a
near-scam event. Typical high-intent searches include:
- how to check if a link is safe
- phishing protection iPhone
- scam link protection
- SMS scam link iPhone
- suspicious link checker


6.4 Launch geography
LinkLook should start with a Netherlands-first launch thesis.

Why Netherlands first:
- manageable market size for trust-sensitive early rollout
- high smartphone and digital-banking usage
- scam awareness already exists
- strong English proficiency reduces localisation pressure
- local press and tech communities can amplify practical security stories fast

Recommended sequence:
- Phase 1: Netherlands
- Phase 2: English-speaking expansion
- Phase 3: broader EU localisation

The Netherlands-first choice is a go-to-market focus, not a product limitation.
The product, website, and App Store assets should remain ready for broader
international reach where practical.

6.5 Dutch launch channels
High-leverage Dutch channels include:
- Tweakers / Dutch tech community
- security-community credibility and expert feedback
- scam-news coverage and consumer-protection press angles
- family-protection narratives around elderly relatives

6.6 Dutch press positioning
Press framing should be human and practical, not “AI security platform.”
Examples:
- Dutch app warns before scam links open
- App helps protect parents from scam links
- iPhone security app checks links before they open
- what happens if you click a phishing link
- protect parents from scams

The app and website copy should align with these phrases in plain English.

6.4 Near-miss growth loop

A strong organic loop exists when LinkLook blocks or warns on a suspicious link:
near miss -> relief -> story -> recommendation -> install

This should be supported carefully with calm UX and optional sharing,
never with loud or self-congratulatory language.

================================================================================
7. COLD-START RISK
================================================================================

The biggest first-year business risk is user inertia.
Most people do not install protection for a risk they have not personally felt.
LinkLook must therefore be designed to win exactly when the user becomes aware
of the problem.

Mitigations:
- free v1
- strong App Store wording
- website pages answering post-scare searches
- family-protection positioning
- visible in-app activity summaries (for example: links checked this week)
- calm explanations that show the app is working even when no threat appeared

================================================================================
8. LONG-TERM ADVANTAGE
================================================================================

If LinkLook logs structured, privacy-minimised technical artefacts from day one,
it can build a valuable dataset of scam infrastructure, redirects, impersonation
patterns, and page classifications at the moment users tap suspicious links.

That dataset supports:
- better detection quality over time
- future SLM training
- stronger threat-intelligence capability
- a durable moat that simple blacklist products do not have

Data platform direction (MDL-102):
The domain reputation database should be exposed via a public API in a future
version, transforming LinkLook from a consumer app into a data platform. The
model includes tiered B2B pricing and positions the database itself as the
defensible acquisition asset. This is distinct from the Certified API Program
(MDL-018/019) which covers universal-link verification.

================================================================================
9. YEAR-ONE MILESTONES
================================================================================

The first year should be judged primarily by these milestones:
1. First 100 real users keep the app installed
2. First confirmed near-miss / interception stories appear
3. Organic sharing begins within families or friend groups
4. Clear product category emerges in user language

Success after year one is not perfection. Success is users being able to say:
“LinkLook warned me before I opened the link.”

================================================================================
10. EXECUTIVE CONCLUSION
================================================================================

LinkLook should be built and launched as a focused, privacy-conscious,
straightforward, security-driven browser product.

The commercial thesis is not “build maximum coverage before launch.”
It is:
- launch a trustworthy product with meaningful protection
- make it easy to discover after a scare
- make it easy to recommend to parents and relatives
- collect evidence and telemetry
- improve protection depth from a position of real-world usage

That path lowers the risk of building an impressive security system with too
few users to matter.
