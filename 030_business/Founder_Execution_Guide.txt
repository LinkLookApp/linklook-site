LINKLOOK — FOUNDER EXECUTION GUIDE
Version: 2026-03-08
Origin: ChatGPT

================================================================================
PURPOSE
================================================================================

This guide turns the March 8, 2026 strategy discussion into founder operating
instructions. It is intentionally practical.

================================================================================
1. THE CURRENT STRATEGIC TRUTH
================================================================================

LinkLook's biggest near-term risk is not technical inferiority.
It is a cold-start adoption problem.

Therefore the first objective is:
Get a trustworthy, understandable, easy-to-try product into the market fast
enough to learn, without creating reputational damage through overclaiming.

================================================================================
2. WHAT TO DO NOW
================================================================================

1. Keep v1 free.
2. Keep the public promise simple: “checks links before they open.”
3. Avoid absolute claims.
4. Build visible but calm proof-of-work into the product.
5. Design discovery around the post-scare search moment.
6. Treat elderly-protection and family-protection as a primary GTM wedge.
7. Keep Network Extension valuable but not sacred.

================================================================================
3. RECOMMENDED PRODUCT DECISION
================================================================================

Default recommendation:
Ship v1 as a straightforward, security-driven browser. Action extension is V2.
Network Extension is V3. Do not let later-version complexity delay first launch.

Reason:
A delayed perfect product is more dangerous than an imperfect but trustworthy
product that starts learning from real usage.

================================================================================
4. WHAT MUST BE TRUE AT LAUNCH
================================================================================

- The app must feel privacy-safe.
- The app must not look like spyware.
- The app must not aggressively block legitimate links at a noticeable rate.
- The app must show some evidence that it is active.
- The App Store page must explain the product in one sentence.
- The website must capture post-scare search intent.
- The product language must be plain English, not security jargon.

================================================================================
5. GROWTH MESSAGES TO USE
================================================================================

Primary messages:
- Checks links before they open
- Protect yourself from scam links
- Protect your parents from scam links
- Reduces the risk of phishing and smishing on iPhone

Messages to avoid:
- Prevents scams
- Total protection
- Full safety guarantee
- Military-grade AI protection

================================================================================
6. FIRST 1000 USERS: WHERE THEY COME FROM
================================================================================

Expected early user sources:
- your network
- security-curious users
- near-scam searchers
- adult children installing for parents
- word-of-mouth after blocked links

The launch plan should be built for these users, not for the mass market.

================================================================================
7. DISCOVERY EXECUTION
================================================================================

Minimum discovery stack:

A. App Store listing
- Name / subtitle must match search intent
- Screenshots must immediately show suspicious-link warning behaviour
- Privacy explanation must be plain and reassuring

B. Website pages
Create simple pages for these search intents:
- how to check if a link is safe on iPhone
- what to do if you tapped a scam link
- SMS scam link iPhone
- protect parents from scams
- phishing protection iPhone

C. Story assets
Have one-page explanations ready for:
- elderly protection
- near-miss stories
- how LinkLook is different from VPN-style products

================================================================================
8. KEY METRICS FOR THE FIRST YEAR
================================================================================

Trust / retention:
- uninstall rate in first 7 days
- first-week active usage
- percentage of users who keep default-browser setting enabled

Product quality:
- false positive rate
- detection quality on known malicious tests
- time to verdict

Growth / learning:
- App Store conversion rate
- organic installs as percentage of total installs
- share of installs tied to family-protection use case
- number of real near-miss stories captured

================================================================================
9. THE THREE REPUTATIONAL DISASTERS TO AVOID
================================================================================

1. Looking like spyware
2. Blocking legitimate sites too often
3. Looking inactive after installation

Every launch decision should be checked against these three risks.

================================================================================
10. WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE IN THE NEXT 6–12 MONTHS
================================================================================

Not revenue first.
Not perfect protection first.

Success means:
- users understand the product instantly
- users keep it installed
- users tell at least one other person about it
- some installs come from search and word-of-mouth rather than only from you
- the first real interception stories arrive

================================================================================
11. FOUNDER RULE
================================================================================

When forced to choose between additional technical depth and earlier trustworthy
market learning, prefer earlier trustworthy market learning — unless the added
technical depth clearly prevents a material safety failure.


================================================================================
12. MARKET FOCUS RULE
================================================================================

Primary launch market remains the Netherlands.

Operating implication:
- local PR first
- Dutch trust narrative first
- Dutch family-protection use case first
- keep product and website internationally portable

Useful Dutch launch surfaces:
- Tweakers and adjacent Dutch tech communities
- Dutch scam-news and consumer-protection coverage
- local stories about protecting elderly relatives from scam links

Press outreach timing to prepare for:
1. first public launch
2. first credible real-world interception story
3. scam-wave moments already in the news
